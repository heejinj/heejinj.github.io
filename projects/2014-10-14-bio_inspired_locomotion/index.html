    Dynamic Legged Locomotion via Formal Control Methods

    Abstract

    Humans and other legged animals are able to walk with exceptional ease and efficiency by utilizing the presence of elasticity in their joints. While such compliance elements are ruled out in many robotic designs and control algorithms, I seek to develop formal control methods that exploit the passive dynamics of the system to achieve natural and efficient bio-inspired locomotion on legged robots. This work is conducted on a highly underactuated bipedal robot, ATRIAS. By extending the existing hybrid zero dynamics (HZD) control framework to systems with high degrees of underactuation, I experimentally realized robust and sustainable walking on ATRIAS that closely emulates the essential concept of walking and running behaviors observed in many animals, from quail to kangaroos, and humans. To tackle the difficulty of the kinematic trajectory optimization problem in our previous approach, I further developed a novel trajectory-free dynamic shaping controller built upon the QP-based control Lyapunov functions (CLFs) to achieve formally stable locomotion that encapsulates the essential dynamics of bio-inspired reduced order model. These works effectively extended the applications of formal control methods to an analytically tricky domain of highly-underactuated robots.
